Abstract

The rapid advancements in text-to-image generation technologies based on diffu- sion models have paved the way for low-cost video editing applications. These methods, built on pretrained models, offer an efficient alternative to training resource-intensive video-specific models. However, achieving temporal consis- tency in these applications remains challenging due to the frame independence of text-to-image generation. Existing methods address this by fine-tuning tem- poral layers or propagating temporal features at inference time, but these ap- proaches either have significant training cost or often fall short in delivering high-quality, temporally coherent video outputs. In this paper, we propose a lightweight, universally applicable adaptor that leverages prompt learning and temporal-aware DDIM inversion to enhance video editing performance across a broad range of text-to-image diffusion-based algorithms with minimal training cost. Our approach introduces shared prompt tokens to improve editing flex- ibility and unshared frame-specific tokens to enforce cross-frame consistency, while the temporal-aware DDIM inversion further refines coherence across video frames. This adaptor seamlessly integrates into existing video editing pipelines, delivering significant improvements in temporal consistency and video quality, benefiting a wide spectrum of downstream video editing algorithms
